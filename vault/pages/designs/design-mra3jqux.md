### Visual References
1. **Braun ET66 Calculator:** Soft-edged, tactile buttons with precise but human layouts that prioritize utility while feeling physically satisfying to touch.
2. **Hand-Bound Linen Notebooks:** Rugged, physical, raw edges, textured spines, and an unpretentious feel of localized, physical ownership.
3. **Analog Synthesizer Modules:** Physical toggle switches, labeled inputs, and compartmentalized control blocks that represent complex internal routing made tangible.

### Typography Rationale
We pair **Albert Sans** (a highly legible, warm, and approachable humanist sans-serif) with **Courier Prime** (a soft-edged, monospaced terminal serif). This pairing evokes the feeling of local files, sovereign codebases, and terminal-level operations, yet avoids the sterile, high-contrast digital look of standard monospace fonts by using Courier's typewriter-like warmth.

### Color Palette
- **Soft Charcoal (#181A18):** A deep, organic green-black mimicking rich loam and damp slate.
- **Earthy Sage (#7A8B7B):** A comforting mid-tone gray-green that softens container boundaries.
- **Raw Clay (#C28469):** A warm, dusty terracotta that highlights system actions and active paths.
- **Off-White Canvas (#F1EFEA):** A bright, soft, non-sterile light tone for copy and high-legibility reads.
- **Mineral Ochre (#CCA250):** An unusual accent color suggesting oxidized hardware and technical highlighting.

### Layout & Motion Strategy
We employ a physical "file folder" card module using asymmetric, rounded offsets and physical borders. Elements look stacked and locally stored rather than floated in an infinite cloud. The system animates with single-axis physical drawer reveals (vertical slides and soft tactile pops) instead of floaty, multi-directional parallax fades.
