# Sovereign Swiss Grid: Design Specification for Greg Iteen

## 1. Concrete Visual References
* **1976 Swiss Federal Railways (SBB) Passenger Timetables**: Pure spatial layout where data density does not compromise legibility. Information is structured purely through position, typographic scale weight, and horizontal spatial datum zones.
* **Dieter Rams' Braun Audio Control Systems (e.g., TS 45, TG 60)**: Pure functional arrangement, stamped crisp technical lettering, high-contrast toggle selectors, and structural division defined by deliberate whitespace and rigorous vertical alignments instead of decorative graphic containers.
* **Wim Crouwel's Archival Exhibition Poster Series for the Stedelijk Museum**: Extreme, unyielding mathematical grids that push typography to its absolute architectural limit. Large-gauge letterforms operating as visual architecture rather than mere text, balanced by micro-metric engineering coordinates.

## 2. Typography Rationale
* **Arimo (Primary Sans)**: A severe, highly technical Neo-Grotesque with micro-engineered metrics. It is unapologetic, highly structural, and strips away all warmth. We pair it here because it perfectly expresses Greg’s focus on robust, sovereign, file-native engineering. It mimics the unyielding functionality of Helvetica and Univers without falling into generic modern-web defaults.
* **Sometype Mono (Technical Mono)**: A clean, rigid, monospaced face designed for high legibility in spatial environments. This is never paired with Arimo on standard commercial websites, as the combination can feel overly clinical and demanding. Here, it is utilized as a raw data locator—acting as a locator tag for metadata, latency tables, file pathways, and structural pagination indicators.

## 3. Color Palette
* **Primary Background**: `#FFFFFF` (Pure clinical white) - The absolute canvas of high-contrast Swiss design. Absolutely no warm cream, ivory, or canvas tints are permitted.
* **Primary Text**: `#000000` (High-density carbon black) - For absolute, uncompromising readability and high graphic density.
* **Accent Color**: `#002FA7` (International Klein / Ultramarine Blue) - The sole color used for operational interfaces, active focus states, functional navigation signals, and interactive markers.
* **Structural Element Tint**: `#F4F5F6` (Technical Slate Grey) - Used for tabular rows, data containers, and background structural blocks that must feel industrial rather than natural.
* **Muted Metadata Label**: `#8F94A0` (Muted Steel) - Used strictly for micro-labels, coordinate paths, and system parameters.

## 4. Layout Strategy & Signature Structural Device
* **The Asymmetric Coordinate Sidebar Grid**: The layout features a persistent left-hand sidebar containing Greg's monogram logo, global site coordinates, and key operational system parameters (such as mock file system statuses). The content area is shifted heavily to the right, sitting on a rigid, multi-column modular grid.
* **The Ultramarine Anchor Datum**: A heavy, 6px horizontal datum bar in `#002FA7` sits at the top of the browser viewport. Every page layout aligns strictly to this starting point, asserting a structural horizon line that organizes all downstream visual hierarchy.
* **High-Contrast Text Blocks**: Links are styled as solid block interactive targets. On hover, they invert instantly—shifting background color to `#002FA7` and text to `#FFFFFF` without transition latency, emphasizing immediate, local-first system execution.

## 5. Self-Critique & Rigor Check
* **No Cliché Traps**: We have rejected near-black with acid-green terminal styles, which are far too common for technical system portfolios. We have also rejected warm cream with editorial serifs. The visual language here is clinical, architectural, and completely distinct. It cannot be mistaken for a standard agency template because of its extreme spatial austerity, pure black-and-white high density layout, and strategic strikes of pure Ultramarine Blue.
